Experience

Find below the details of my research career, starting from wet-lab roots, my skills have evolved towards being able to support teams of researchers with their computational needs.

Computational Biologist

Sequence Bioinformatics Inc.

Sequence Bio is a St. John's, NL based company with a focus on making genetic discoveries, leveraging data from the NL Genome Project to make novel discoveries based on the collective genetics of the Newfoundland and Labrador founder population.

  • Put together population genetics pipelines with a focus on scalability and reproducibility
  • Derived disease associations by analyzing the company's NL population cohort
  • Combined company and public datasets to derive insights into the NL population's genetic features

Research Programmer

BC Genome Sciences Centre

BC Genome Sciences Centre is a Vancouver, BC based research centre with numerous laboratories with varied topics, conducting world-leading research into all things genomics: from cancer to vascular disease all the developing software for others to use. The GSC is integrated into BC's Provincial Health Services Authority, delivering critical health services to the BC population.

  • Performed quality control of incoming clinical oncopanel wet lab data through NGS data quality metrics
  • Streamlines existing quality control and visualization scripts and configured for reusability
  • Assisted graduate students with applying computational aproaches to research questions

Biosensor R&D Programmer

CiBER Laboratory, Simon Fraser University

The Centre for Integrative Bio-Engineering Research, headed by Dr. Kaminska is a research facility within Simon Fraser University with the goal of developing materials and devices that help bridge our understanding of biology. From sensors to microfluidics and access to microfabrication techniques the CiBER team is researching leading material science advances.

  • Implemented sensor data processing suite for generating easy to understand visualizations
  • Performed collection of data from hundreds of sensor instance during development
  • Presented and implemented metrics for determining sensor instance success based on sensor response patterns

Genetics Co-op Student

Aziz Sancar Institute of Experimental Medicine, Istanbul University

Laboratory Number 2, headed by Dr. Ekmekci, is at the crossroads of clinical study and R&D efforts of the Aziz Sancar Institute. Responsible for providing facilities for performing clinical diagnostics and conducting leading research into clinical foundings via sequencing and cell culture methods.

  • Carried out PCR, DNA and RNA sample isolations from clinical samples and performed sample QC
  • Maintained populations of five widely used cancer cell lines in cell culture laboratory
  • Lead and supported four other co-op students during their initial time at the lab
